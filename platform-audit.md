# LexiPrep Premium Feature Audit

Research checked April 2026 against LSAC/LawHub, 7Sage, LSAT Demon, LSAT Lab, Blueprint, Magoosh, and Khan Academy/LawHub resources.

## Current LSAT Requirements

- Multiple choice LSAT: Logical Reasoning and Reading Comprehension only, with four 35-minute sections: three scored and one unscored.
- LSAT Argumentative Writing is separate, online, unscored, and required for score release if no valid writing sample is on file.
- The app should avoid Logic Games as a core study lane and focus on LR, RC, timing, writing, and score-release readiness.

## Personal Design Rules for Jessica

- Default to short study sprints: lesson, 6-question drill, one journal note, stop.
- Keep answer choice reading slow and line-by-line; do not force speed before structure.
- Use visible task labels before answer choices: conclusion, flaw, assumption, weaken, role, RC structure.
- Treat skipped questions as strategy, not failure.
- Use the wrong-answer journal to record one plain-language rule, not a long paragraph.
- Pair every video lesson with a drill immediately.

## Missing Next-Level Upgrades

1. Pattern-level analytics: causal flaw, sampling flaw, comparison gap, quantifier trap, scope shift, tone trap.
2. Pacing analytics: time per question, skipped/returned, final-five accuracy, RC passage time split.
3. Full mock-test mode: three scored sections plus one experimental section, break screen, and scaled-score estimate.
4. Writing practice: 15-minute prewrite, 35-minute essay, perspective checklist, rubric.
5. Calendar automations: real reminders for live classes, timed tests, and spaced review.
6. Printable dyslexia-friendly cheat sheets through Canva.
7. Import/export to Google Sheets for score tracking.
8. A LawHub companion page that tells Jessica exactly which official PrepTest or drill to do next.

## Content Expansion Rule

The local question bank can use original LSAT-style material. Official LSAT questions and copyrighted prep-book text should not be copied into the app. Use LawHub or licensed prep platforms for official questions, then log results and mistake patterns inside LexiPrep.
